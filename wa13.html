Introduction

For many generations, the stock market, public trading, penny stocks, and crypto currencies have been signified as a world in itself. This world has always been one that invites a select group of privileged investors to make money hand over foot, behind closed doors. However, with current advances in technological mediums and platforms, trading in the stock market has been opened to an entirely new class of investors. Unfortunately for these private, experienced investors this means having to adapt and conform to trends they have never seen before, as unlikely and foolish as they may inherently seem. Take Dogecoin, for example, not only was it created as a parody, but there is little material value to the currency itself other than investor inflation. Yet, it is these fundamental characteristics that makes these new trends so interesting to invest in and learn from.

Doge Coin

Doge Coin is a particularly interesting cryptocurrency because of its creation and resulting influence from online social circles. Speculation for these types of stocks is always influenced by major trends from these new age investors. Recently, there has been a lot of interest in Doge Coin. Perhaps from small social circles wanting to capitalize on larger crypto investing trends, or from singular influential figureheads like Elon Musk. Not much can be said about what exactly started this shift, but it is being seen more and more in a variety of areas. Below you can see one of these large increases due to these differing social influences such as Twitter, Coinbase, and endorsements from celebrities like Elon Musk.

Dogecoin Opening Price 2021
 Above is a visual representation of the general increase in opening stock prices for Dogecoin. At the start of the year little change or any increase was seen in Dogecoin's opening price, yet an increase in crypto currency investment and general social hype over "DogeDay", April 20, created this large surge in crypto stock price.
