A Little About Me

I graduated from Grandview High School, but initially started at Regis Jesuit. During much of my life I played lacrosse, ten years actually. Beyond this, I played a variety of other sports including water polo and tennis. Sports and athletic activities were a foundational part of my life, and built who I am today. Even though being active is a large part of my life, the majority of my time is dedicated to more technological mediums. After starting as a Computer Science major at the University of Colorado Boulder I quickly realized how much I enjoyed coding and learning how computers handle increasingly complicated problems. I later shifted my major to Information Science, finding that it increasingly allowed me to pursue coding and other interesting tools in a variety of topical environments or subject areas. This new major allowed me to gain extensive experience in python and corresponding python libraries, conducting in-depth data aggregation, cleaning, and offering quanitative analysis or insights. Some specific libraries that I have consistently been exposed to include Pandas, Numpy, MatPlotLib, Altair, Seaborn, and many others. These tools, in turn, allowed me to pursue other interesting areas such as experience in minor web development with HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Bootstrap, and hosting Github repositories. Im continually finding that I love working with these tools, and hope to use them in the future. Below you can find a copy of my resume as well as examples of my previous graphic design experience. To find some examples of what I have been currently working on, please visit my portfolio page!
