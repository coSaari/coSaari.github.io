Breaking Bad Viewing Analysis

Overview

  The show Breaking Bad has been popularized since its captivating season finale release. The relatability of the characters and the inclusion of platforms like Netflix, allowed this television show to become cemented in foundational media history. Breaking Bad became one of most talked about shows primarily because of its circumstantial place in time. During its airing, streaming services began to become more popularized and mainstream. Gaining viewers at rates unseen since the creation of the television and radio. This analysis will quantitatively show how a streaming service like Netflix can exponentially increase the amount of people that watch a particular show, in this case, Breaking Bad.

Analysis

  To obtain a better understanding of how well Netflix boosted Breaking Bad’s viewership, I had to find a dataset pertaining to what I needed. One interesting dataset I ended up utilizing was from Kaggle This dataset is in CSV format, and is an aggregated source for us-viewer numbers per episode, as well as particular rating and voting data. Initially, I created several visualizations to help me understand what the data was attempting to show. I ended up using Altair formatting to create this first graph that shows the number of votes from the movie rating site Ratingraph, per episode. This shows a very basic understanding of how exponentially increasing the number of votes for each episode is for this particular online community. Beyond, recognizing this initial trend, I wanted to see how this smaller group of avid movie watchers related to the general US population watching. This next graph shows the exponential growth in the number of US viewers per episode of Breaking Bad. Again, this increase can be clearly seen with the increase in episodes through the utilizationg of regression lines. Within both visualizations these regression lines at degrees 1 and 3 expresses the relationship between the response variables y (votes / us-viewers) and the explanatory variable (episodes). As the episodes increase throughout the life of the series, this linear increase can be seen both in the total number of votes and total number of US viewers. To clarify this relationship further, this third graph shows the strong correlation between the number of US viewers per episode and the number of votes per episode. This scatter plot matrix shows the varying relationships between our three main variable, with correlation being shown across the diagnal. These visualizations represent the major aspects of the large increase in viewership Breaking Bad saw as a result of its incorporation into Netflix's streaming platform. Not only, did it see national increases in popularity, but at it also saw these increases in local levels of media enthusiasm as well.






As Netflix continually began to advertise and allow people to easily stream the show, its following and popularity grew. Statistically speaking this is very apparent. Research

  Beyond recognition of this quantitative increase in viewership through statistical analysis, many articles and sources have also seen this magnification effect by Netflix. One article from Vox in particular saw this trend and fittingly named it the “Netflix Effect”. Or as they put it, “Netflix’s ability to find new and bigger audiences for shows”. Some reasons for this effect is that streaming services allow for particular characteristics that viewers inherently look for. Such as limited viewing of advertisements, continual streaming of multiple episodes, and ease of access. One CNBC article, also pointed out the shear magnitude of this increase in viewership by stating “it has the third most watched series finale in cable history, behind only HBO’s “The Sopranos” and “Sex & the City”. The creator of the show Vince Gilligan even shared at the Golden Globes how “Netflix saved Breaking Bad”. Prior to the real advent of multiple streaming services, Netflix had a majority control of the market and this allowed for network shows and Netflix original shows hosted on Netflix to have a lot of users watching at one time. However, as time has gone on there are now many more services that are moving towards streaming, and with the recent pandemic many have been changing the way they watch TV. According to USA Today , “Netflix and Hulu claimed a smaller share of viewing than they did last year...Netflix claimed 29% of total streaming minutes...down from 36% the previous year”. The more viewers move away from Netflix, the more other streaming platforms begin to grow, for example, “Youtube represented 20% up from 19%, Amazon inched up to 9% from 7%, and other services like Disney Plus and Apple TV jumped to 31% from 23%”. Even though this market has become saturated with competing organizations, the overall trend towards streaming as the primary source of televised entertainment is apparent.

Conclusion

  The television show Breaking Bad is a fundamental example of the role streaming platforms have played in popularizing television. Not only did the advent of Netflix boost this shows viewers to historic levels, but streaming as a whole realized the evolving expectations of the general user. As more and more media have moved to online platforms, niche markets for larger genres such as news, television, radio, and music will be created and expanded at exponential rates. The real question is whether you can recognize this shift or trend, and create a product that fits with this general transition to better information sharing and consumption.
