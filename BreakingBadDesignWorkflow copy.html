Learning

  •   The show Breaking Bad has been popularized since its captivating season finale release. The relatability of the characters and the inclusion of platforms like Netflix, allowed this television show to become cemented in foundational media history. Breaking Bad became one of most talked about shows primarily because of its circumstantial place in time. During its airing, streaming services began to become more popularized and mainstream. Gaining viewers at rates unseen since the creation of the television and radio. The primary problem I plan to focus on in this design workflow is how the viewership of Breaking Bad increased over its lifetime to explain the inclusion of online streaming services. This problem is crucial in understand how exponentially increasing viewing numbers can be achieved as the result of a new innovative medium.

  •   To obtain a better understanding of how well Netflix boosted Breaking Badâ€™s viewership, I had to find a dataset pertaining to what I needed. This dataset also includes interesting metadata included in the kaggle dataset, such as air date, season / episode number. Granted there is little information regarding how the us-viewer numbers were aggregated and if it includes multiple sources of viewers. For consistency, particular episodes in season two had to be dropped because of NULL values in certain feature characteristics


Core

Discover

  Task 1: Show how US viewer numbers increased as more episodes were produced.

  • Why(goal): Pursued in order to present how general television users were influenced.
  • How(means): This will be conducted by comparing or relating the US viewership and each episode in each season.
  • What(characteristics): This will observe both low-level features such as US viewers, and high-level logistical trends.
  • Where(target data): This will operate in specifically a temporal / spatial relation, in that the episodes are chronologically and temporally spaced to the views.
  • When(workflow): Conducted prior to deducing insights based on general trends and after collection of prevalent data.
  • Who(roles): This specifies user's that hold direct interest in television trends or media viewing history.


  Task 2: Show how rating and voting numbers increased as more episodes were produced.

  • Why(goal): Presentation on how avid television critics were influenced by the shows popularity.
  • How(means): Conducted by relating the voting and rating data to the sequential order of episodes.
  • What(characteristics): Observing low-level features such as voting / rating data and high-level logistical trends.
  • Where(target data): Operating in a temporal / spatial relation given the episodic construction.
  • When(workflow): Conducted prior to deducing insights based on general trends and after collection of prevalent data.
  • Who(roles): This specifies user's that hold direct interest in television trends or media viewing history.

A vertical bar chart was also utilized in order to better frame the increase in viewership.

    • This first iteration of the second task outlines the comparisons between the total votes as well as ratings and the number of episodes per season of Breaking Bad. As well, a categorical color scheme was utilized in order to better portray the differing increasing seasons.

    Implement

      

    • The implementation of the tasks incorporates two major design choices. The first being the inclusion of an interactive tool tip that provides the user with all the meta-data needed to understand each episode's value. Furthermore, overlayed regression lines at specific degree intervals, with categorical coloring, offers that high-value data characteristic needed to make specifc inferences.



    Deploy

      Task 1

    • This first task visualization shows the relationship between the number of episodes per season of Breaking Bad and the total number of US viewers. Some particular observations that were of interest was the inconsistent exponential increase in the number of votes and the number of views per episode. One would expect that the highest voted episode was the season finale, but in reality it was the second to last episode that was voted for the most.

      Task 2

    • This second task visualization shows the relationships of votes and ratings against the number of episodes. One observation that was very apparent was the little deviation amongst the particular ratings per episode. There is a slight increase as the seasons increase, but this can most likely be explained by the increase in general viewers as the show became more popular. However, the number of votes per episode also correlates well with the number of US viewers per episode above.


    Iterate

      

    • Primarily for the tasks above, I believe that the inclusion of meta-data from other similar movie or television ratings sites would be very crucial to put this portion of data into perspective. Even though the US viewers and votes/ratings are from differing third party datasets. The votes/ratings only offers the perspective of one specific review site. With multiple features from varying datasets, I believe that the clarity of this general trend would be even more magnifiable
